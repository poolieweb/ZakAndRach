How to get there

Cortijo Sabila is 30 minutes north from the costa so we really recommend hiring a car or hitching a ride with someone. For those people who are staying at the Cortijo and are not driving we can organise for a taxi (or Zak to pick you up) so that you can have a nice relaxing journey.

On the day

On the big day there will be ample free parking for the designated drivers (or those leaving their car overnight) and the venue can supply taxis for those staying outside of the cortijo (just let us know and we can arrange it for you!).

What to do

The venue is located in Andalucia, a beautiful part of Spain with so much to see and explore so we are hoping there is something for everyone; whether you want to get out and see it all, or enjoy a well earned break by the pool.

The area is rural but has good road links to nearby areas including; Malaga, Granada, Cordoba, Seville and Ronda and now that data roaming is free (wahey!) getting around has never been easier.

    Things to see nearby

  • Antequera (20 mins)

    The nearest large town and is a charming Roman town which is free of those annoying English tourists!

  • El Torcal (30 mins)

    For those who feel a bit more active there is the stunning Torcal National Park which offers a couple of trails through unusual rock formations

  • Malaga (30 mins)

    Malaga has much to see, eat and drink. We would recommend the old town for a bit of exploring and tapas (maybe even some churros)

  • Beaches (30-60 mins)

    There are loads of beaches and ports on the costa del sol, take your pick

  • Caminito Del Rey (60 mins)

    An 8km walk along the “King’s Little Pathway”. The walk follows several gorges and it not for the faint hearted. If interested aim to pre-book as often sells out in advance link

    • A little further away

  • Granada (60 mins)

    Alhambra palace, Medieval complex housing palaces, carved Moorish throne room & cloistered courtyards with fountains.

  • Seville (2h)

    Seville is the capital of southern Spain’s Andalucia region. It's famous for flamenco dancing, particularly in its Triana neighborhood

  • Cordoba (1h 20)

    Cordoba’s main attraction is the dazzling Mezquita but it has the growing reputation as Spain’s gastronomic capital with lots of tempting tapas and wine bars. A great place for exploring by foot or bike.

  • Ronda (1h 20)

    Ronda is a mountaintop city in Spain’s Malaga province that’s set dramatically above a deep gorge. This gorge (El Tajo) separates the city’s circa-15th-century new town from its old town, dating to Moorish rule
